Emotional Intelligence

Sprint 3 Core Document

What is emotional intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence is being able to recognise and comprehend your own and other people's emotions and behaviours and to utilise that understanding to help you navigate experiences, relationships, and situations.


How is it different to IQ?

It's used to understand social and emotional behaviours, or the ability to recognise and identify emotions in oneself and others, rather than intellectual and academic ability.


Why is it important?

Emotional intelligence may improve many aspects of your life, it is useful for more than just the workplace. Understanding and regulating your emotions can improve your view on life and make you feel better about yourself and your circumstances. Speaking with someone about a situation that makes you feel a certain emotion can help you see things from a different perspective.
